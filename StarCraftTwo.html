Summary

StarCraft II is the sequel to the iconic StarCraft series, released by Blizzard Entertainment in 2010. The game is split into three separate installments, each focusing on a different faction's storyline, and features an expanded narrative, new gameplay mechanics, and enhanced graphics. It continues the saga of the Terrans, Zerg, and Protoss in the Koprulu Sector.

Key Installments

StarCraft II: Heart of the Swarm (2013):

  • Focus: The Terran campaign follows Jim Raynor, a rebel leader, as he tries to overthrow the corrupt Terran Dominion led by Arcturus Mengsk.
  • Story: Raynor battles against both the Dominion and the Zerg, uncovering a larger conspiracy involving an ancient enemy, Amon. The Terran forces fight to stop the Zerg infestation and protect humanity from impending doom.
  • Gameplay: Players control Raynor's forces, building bases, gathering resources, and commanding armies in strategic real-time battles.

StarCraft II: Heart of the Swarm (2013):

  • Focus: The Zerg campaign centers around Sarah Kerrigan, the former Terran ghost who was transformed into the Zerg Queen, after being betrayed by Mengsk.
  • Story: Kerrigan seeks revenge on Mengsk and reclaim her humanity while leading the Zerg swarm to victory. She also struggles with the influence of the dark power within her and faces new threats from an ancient alien force, Amon.
  • Gameplay: Players control Kerrigan's forces, with a focus on Zerg units and unique abilities. The game expands on the Zerg's gameplay mechanics, emphasizing swarm tactics and strategic evolution.

StarCraft II: Legacy of the Void (2015):

  • Focus: The Protoss campaign focuses on Artanis, the Hierarch of the Protoss, as he seeks to reunite the fractured Protoss factions and lead them against the Zerg and the greater threat of Amon.
  • Story: Artanis must unite the Protoss people, defeat the Zerg, and stop Amon from destroying the galaxy. This installment delves into the ancient history of the Protoss and their struggle for survival.
  • Gameplay: Players control Protoss forces, with an emphasis on advanced technology and powerful units. The Protoss' focus on high-tech, strategic play contrasts with the other factions.

Gameplay

StarCraft II retains the core mechanics of the original StarCraft, including resource gathering, base-building, and strategic real-time combat. However, it refines these elements with modern graphics, new units, and abilities for each faction. The game's multiplayer mode is a major highlight, featuring a competitive ladder system, ranked matches, and a robust esports scene.

New Units and Mechanics

Terran Additions

  • Hellions/Hellbats: Fast light vehicles that can transform between modes for different tactical situations.
  • Thors: Massive mechanical walkers with anti-air and anti-ground capabilities.
  • Widow Mines: Burrowing units that deal massive splash damage to enemies.
  • MULEs: Temporary mining units called down by Orbital Commands to boost economy.

    • Zerg Additions

  • Roaches: Armored ground units with regeneration and burrowing abilities.
  • Banelings: Suicide units that deal devastating area damage to ground units.
  • Corruptors: Anti-air flying units that can transform into Brood Lords.
  • Swarm Hosts: Units that spawn free locusts to harass enemies.

    • Protoss Additions

  • Stalkers: Mobile ranged units with Blink ability for tactical positioning.
  • Colossi: Massive walkers with thermal lances that deal splash damage.
  • Phoenixes: Fast air units with graviton beams to lift enemy units.
  • Oracles: Fast harassment units with revelation and time warp abilities.

    • Esports and Competitive Scene

    StarCraft II became one of the biggest esports titles, with tournaments like the Global StarCraft II League (GSL), World Championship Series (WCS), and Intel Extreme Masters featuring professional players competing for substantial prize pools. The game's balanced design and high skill ceiling made it a favorite among competitive gamers worldwide, particularly in South Korea where StarCraft had already established a massive following.

    Legacy

    StarCraft II was praised for its captivating storytelling, polished mechanics, and balance. It further solidified StarCraft as one of the most influential RTS franchises. The game has a strong esports presence, especially in Korea, and has maintained a dedicated fanbase with its expansions and updates. The trilogy concluded the story arc started in the original StarCraft, bringing closure to many character arcs and setting the stage for future installments (if any).
