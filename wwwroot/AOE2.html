Summary

Age of Empires II (1999) is a landmark real-time strategy (RTS) game developed by Ensemble Studios. Set during the medieval period, it builds upon the gameplay foundations established in the first Age of Empires but offers a more refined experience with improved graphics, more civilizations, and deeper strategic mechanics. It is widely considered one of the greatest RTS games of all time.

Key Features

  • Setting: The game takes place during the Middle Ages, covering a wide range of civilizations from Europe, Asia, Africa, and the Americas. It spans from the fall of the Roman Empire through to the Renaissance.
      • Civilizations: Age of Empires II introduces 13 civilizations, each with unique units, technologies, and abilities. Notable examples include:
    • Britons: Known for their strong archers.
    • Franks: Powerful cavalry and a strong economy.
    • Saracens: Focused on naval superiority and unique units like the Mamelukes.
    • Vikings: Strong naval forces and an emphasis on longships.
    • Aztecs: A Mesoamerican civilization with powerful infantry and priests.
      Gameplay:
    • Resource Management: Players collect resources such as food, wood, gold, and stone to build structures, create units, and research technologies.
    • Base Building and Expansion: Players construct buildings, train armies, and advance through four primary ages: Dark Age, Feudal Age, Castle Age, and Imperial Age. Advancing to higher ages unlocks more advanced units, technologies, and the ability to expand your territory.
    • Combat and Strategy: The game is known for its tactical depth, with combat involving a variety of units, including infantry, cavalry, archers, siege weapons, and naval forces. Each unit has strengths and weaknesses, and players must use them strategically to outmaneuver their opponents.
    • Victory Conditions: Players can win by defeating all enemies, completing specific objectives, or controlling all important resources on the map. Some modes offer alternative victory conditions like building a wonder or achieving technological supremacy.
      Campaign and Story:
    • The game features four single-player campaigns, each telling a historical story
    • The Rise of the Huns: Follows Attila the Hun and his conquests.
    • Joan of Arc: Focuses on the famous French heroine's battle against the English.
    • Saladin: The story of the Muslim leader during the Crusades.
    • The Mongol Empire: Chronicles the rise of Genghis Khan and the Mongol conquests.
    • These campaigns often feature historical scenarios and include a mix of combat, economy, and strategic challenges.

    Story and Campaign

    The game includes various campaigns based on historical events and figures. Players can follow the rise and fall of civilizations, such as the rise of the Egyptian empire, the conquest of the Hittites, or the struggles of the Romans.

    Advanced Civilizations and Unique Units

    Civilization Details

    • Britons: Unique Unit: Longbowman (archer with exceptional range). Strong at defensive and archer strategies.
    • Franks: Unique Unit: Throwing Axeman (infantry). Cavalry and knight rush strategies are highly effective.
    • Goths: Unique Unit: Huskarl (infantry resistant to archers). Excel at infantry spam and late-game pressure.
    • Mongols: Unique Unit: Mangudai (cavalry archer). Dominant in cavalry archer compositions and mobile warfare.
    • Chinese: Unique Unit: Chu Ko Nu (rapid-fire archer). Start with extra villagers but less resources.
    • Byzantines: Unique Unit: Cataphract (heavy cavalry). Strong defensive civilization with good all-around bonuses.
    • Koreans: Unique Unit: War Wagon (cavalry archer with high defense). Superior siege weapons and tower range.

    Military Units and Counter Strategies

    Unit Types

    • Infantry: Militia line, Spearman line, Eagle Warriors. Effective against buildings and some cavalry.
    • Archers: Archer line, Skirmisher line, Cavalry Archers. Strong against infantry, weak to cavalry.
    • Cavalry: Scout line, Knight line, Camel line. Fast and powerful against archers and siege.
    • Siege Weapons: Rams, Catapults, Trebuchets. Essential for destroying buildings and formations.
    • Naval Units: Fire Ships, Galleons, Cannon Galleons. Critical for water map control.

    Counter System

    Understanding the rock-paper-scissors counter system is essential: Infantry counter buildings and some cavalry, Archers counter infantry, Cavalry counters archers and siege, Spearmen counter cavalry, and Skirmishers counter archers. Mastering these relationships is key to victory.

    Economic Strategies

    Efficient resource management is crucial. Key economic strategies include:

    • Fast Castle: Rush to Castle Age with minimal military for faster technology access.
    • Boom: Focus on villager production and economy before military pressure.
    • Feudal Rush: Apply early military pressure in Feudal Age with archers or scouts.
    • Trade: Establish trade routes with markets for gold generation in late game.

    Legacy

    Age of Empires II has remained incredibly popular due to its refined gameplay, deep strategic elements, and variety of civilizations. It has had multiple expansions, including:

  • The Conquerors (2000), which introduced new civilizations and campaigns.
  • Age of Empires II: Definitive Edition (2019), a remastered version with updated graphics, new content, and quality-of-life improvements.

    • The game also has a thriving multiplayer community, with a competitive scene and dedicated players around the world. Its legacy endures through its continued popularity and influence on later RTS games.
