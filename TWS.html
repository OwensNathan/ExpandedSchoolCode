Summary

The Total War series, developed by Creative Assembly, is a highly acclaimed series of real-time strategy (RTS) games known for combining large-scale tactical battles with deep turn-based strategy and empire management. The series is notable for its detailed historical settings, complex systems of diplomacy, and epic, real-time combat that allows players to control thousands of units on the battlefield.

Key Features

  • Hybrid Gameplay: The Total War games combine turn-based strategy for managing empires and diplomacy with real-time tactical battles. Players spend most of their time in a turn-based campaign mode, where they manage resources, diplomacy, and military movements, then switch to real-time battles to resolve conflicts.
  • Historical Setting: The series covers a wide range of historical periods, including the Ancient, Medieval, Renaissance, and Napoleonic eras. Each game offers a different historical backdrop, and the gameplay mechanics reflect the era's technologies, units, and strategies.
  • Empire Management: Players control a faction or civilization and must manage territories, economies, resources, and diplomacy. This includes building cities, recruiting armies, negotiating alliances, and dealing with rebellions, economic issues, and other challenges.
  • Real-Time Battles: The hallmark of Total War is its large-scale real-time battles. Players command thousands of units, including infantry, cavalry, archers, and siege weapons, on expansive battlefields. Tactics such as flanking, terrain advantages, morale, and unit positioning are key to victory. The battles are highly detailed, with units behaving realistically and a strong emphasis on historical accuracy.
  • Diplomacy and Politics: Diplomacy plays a crucial role in Total War games. Players negotiate with other factions for alliances, trade agreements, and other political arrangements. Diplomacy and relationships can influence the broader strategic environment, affecting the likelihood of war, peace, or betrayal.

      Shogun: Total War (2000):
    • Setting: Set in feudal Japan during the Sengoku period, Shogun was the first game in the series. It focused on the rise of powerful clans vying for control of Japan.
    • Legacy: Introduced the core mechanics of managing an empire in a turn-based system and resolving battles in real time.
      Medieval: Total War (2002):
    • Setting: Set in the Middle Ages, Medieval covers Europe, the Middle East, and parts of Asia. It introduced complex diplomatic systems and focused on both military and religious elements.
    • Legacy: Expanded on the franchise by introducing multi-layered mechanics such as crusades, religious influence, and new unit types.
      Rome: Total War (2004):
    • Setting: Set in ancient Rome, players could control the Roman Republic or any of several other factions, including Gaul, Egypt, and the Seleucid Empire.
    • Legacy: Often considered one of the best entries in the series, it introduced 3D graphics, making battles more immersive. It also featured a large map, complex faction dynamics, and historically accurate Roman military units.

    Legacy and Impact

    The Total War series is one of the most well-known RTS franchises, praised for its balance between strategic empire management and large-scale tactical battles. Its mix of turn-based gameplay for strategic planning and real-time battles has set it apart from other RTS games. The series is beloved by strategy enthusiasts for its depth, historical accuracy, and replayability, and it has had a major influence on the development of other RTS titles.

    The Total War series also sparked a thriving modding community, with many mods available that extend the gameplay and add new historical settings. Each new entry continues to evolve the core formula, with the developers incorporating fan feedback and technological advancements to maintain the series' popularity.
